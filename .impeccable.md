## Design Context

### Users
General public in Guadalajara, Jalisco discovering the restaurant — locals and travelers alike. They land on the site wanting to find practical info fast: where is it, when is it open, what's on the menu, is it worth a visit. Context is typically mobile, often decided on in the moment ("¿dónde comemos chamorro?").

### Brand Personality
Distrito Chamorro is a restaurant in Guadalajara where *el chamorro* (pork shank) is the protagonist. It blends Guadalajara tradition with urban execution — powerful consomé, hand-made tortillas, barro de Tonalá, flavor without pretension. Three-word personality: **rooted, generous, unpretentious**. Voice is confident Mexican Spanish, Guadalajara-inflected, never precious. Speaks like a cook who knows exactly what they do well and doesn't oversell.

### Aesthetic Direction
**Urban-Mexican hybrid, editorial warmth.** Tradition grounded in Tonalá ceramics and Jalisco cantina culture, executed with contemporary typographic confidence. Think: if a Guadalajara chamorrería ran its identity through a serious design studio without losing a single gram of earthiness. Warm, light theme — the color of fresh masa, nixtamal, and barro cocido. References: modern Mexican restaurant identities that let ingredients and craft speak, not gimmicky "Mexican-themed" graphic tropes (no cactus clipart, no sombrero silhouettes, no neon agave).

**Anti-references**: template restaurant sites (hero carousel, card grid of dishes, generic booking widget), "rustic chalkboard" kitsch, Inter-on-white SaaS blandness, cliched Mexican fiesta palettes.

### Design Principles
1. **El producto primero** — the chamorro, the consomé, the tortilla are the protagonists. Design serves them, not the other way around.
2. **Tipografía como voz** — typography does the talking. Oversized display, confident sizing, Spanish copy treated with editorial weight.
3. **Tierra, no gris** — every neutral is tinted warm (toward barro). No cool grays, no pure black, no pure white.
4. **Barro de Tonalá** — the palette is derived from clay and fire: terracotta, nixtamal cream, saffron consomé, deep nopal green. Dominant warms with sparing color punctuation.
5. **Sin pretensión** — no decorative effects for their own sake. Every detail earns its place. Warm, generous, and confident, never precious.

Spanish-only (Mexican, Guadalajara voice).
